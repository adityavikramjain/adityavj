Prompt Engineering 101: Structure Matters

The Principle: Garbage In, Garbage Out. A good prompt has structure.

(R) Role: "Act as a..." (e.g., B2B Sales Analyst)
(C) Context: "I am trying to..." (e.g., Prepare for a meeting with a CIO)
(T) Task: "Identify/Analyze/Draft..." (e.g., 3 key priorities based on this data)

Step 2: Analyze with R.C.T. Framework

Now take the Gemini research output and extract actionable sales intelligence.

His top 3 strategic priorities right now. 2. His likely KPIs and financial concerns. 3. Two specific, non-salesy conversation starters based *only* on this data. Ask the Audience: "Would you respond to this?"

Treat AI Like a Brilliant Intern

The Principle: AI is a brilliant intern, not a mind reader.

It needs:

  • Specific instructions
  • Clear context
  • Strict guardrails

The Fix:

To get professional output, you must provide professional input.

The Precision Outreach Framework: R.C.S.T.C.

We upgrade the R.C.T. framework by adding Signal and Constraints.

(R) Role: Who are you?
(C) Context: What are you doing?
(S) Signal (NEW): Why are you reaching out NOW? (The insight from Module 1)
(T) Task: What is the output?
(C) CONSTRAINTS (NEW): What should the AI absolutely NOT do? (The secret sauce)

Scenario 1: The High-Stakes Connection

Context: Arjun (MedTech Manager) connecting with the CFO, Mr. Rao.

The Signal (From Module 1): Mr. Rao is prioritizing "optimizing cash flow for the new cardiology wing."

Switch to Live Screen Share.

The Precision Outreach Prompt

R.C.S.T.C. Prompt
(R) Role: Act as Arjun, an experienced Regional Manager (10+ yrs exp). (C) Context: Sending a cold LinkedIn connection request to Mr. Rao, the new CFO. Goal is long-term relationship building. (S) Signal: He is focused on optimizing cash flow for the new cardiology wing (Source: Annual Report). (T) Task: Draft the connection request (max 300 characters). (C) Constraints: - Tone: Peer-to-peer, professional, concise. - CRITICAL: DO NOT mention the company name or the product. - CRITICAL: DO NOT ask for a meeting. - Start by acknowledging his specific priority.

Execute the prompt live.

The Power of Constraints

Strategic Output

The Takeaway: Constraints (the 'DO NOTs') are the secret to forcing the AI to be strategic, not salesy.

Module 3

The AI Content Engine

Building Credibility at Speed

Content is Not About Going Viral

The Purpose: It's about ensuring that when you reach out (Module 2), the prospect finds a credible presence when they Google you.

It's a Tiebreaker:

When products are similar, credibility wins.

The AI Content Paradox

The Challenge: You have deep expertise, but you don't have time to write.
The AI Reality: AI writes quickly, but it has zero real-world experience.
The Solution: You provide the expertise; AI provides the structure and speed.

Content Strategy Guardrails

Regulated Industries

(Pharma, Finance, Medical Devices)

Focus: Disease awareness, data analysis, leadership.

Constraint: Never talk about specific products or make claims.

B2B Industries

(Tech, Industrial, Services)

Focus: Customer problems, insights, lessons learned.

Constraint: Don't post about your features; post about your customer's problems.
